Main problems of the terrestrial ecosystem

The main problems

Land ecosystem

The terrestrial ecosystem is seriously impacted every day by human activities. Many natural processes are disrupted, leading to loss of biodiversity and a deterioration in the quality of life on the planet.

Deforestation

Mass deforestation deprives animals of their homes, reduces oxygen levels, and exacerbates climate change.

Details

Deforestation

Forests are the "lungs of the planet." Mass deforestation leads to: a decrease in oxygen; the disappearance of animal habitats; a disruption of the climate balance.

Deserts

Desertification

Fertile lands are gradually turning into deserts due to deforestation, drought, and improper soil use.

Details

Desertification

Due to improper land use, fertile soils are turning into deserts. This reduces the area suitable for agriculture and threatens food security.

Soil

Soil pollution

Chemicals, plastic, and waste end up in the soil, making it unfit for plants. This harms ecosystems.

Details

Soil pollution

Chemical waste, pesticides, and garbage enter the soil, making it unsuitable for plants and dangerous for living organisms.

Animals

Extinction of species

Due to the loss of natural habitats, many animals and plants are on the verge of extinction. Each extinct species disrupts the balance of nature

Details

Extinction of species

Many animals and plants are on the verge of extinction due to the destruction of natural habitats and climate change.

Climate

Climate change

Rising temperatures and extreme weather events are destroying natural environments. This affects all terrestrial ecosystems.

Details

Climate change

Rising temperatures, droughts, and extreme weather conditions are negatively affecting all terrestrial ecosystems.
